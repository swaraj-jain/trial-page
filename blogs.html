- Nishad Mandlik(2019 Batch)

×

“Apna safar, club ke is logo se lekar club ke in logon tak raha”. This single line, which was said by one of my RoboISM batchmates during our last club meet, accurately describes my entire journey with the club. From having a club without even a logo to seeing that same club bag gold at Inter-IIT Tech Meet, from working with a team of fewer than 15 members to growing into a 40 member strong fraternity, I consider myself fortunate for being able to witness and be a part of this evolution.

Going back to 2015, a couple of months after I joined ISM Dhanbad, I had attended a basic robotics workshop organized by the robotics club. My seniors were demonstrating the blinking of an LED, using Arduino Uno. It does sound stupid, but that’s how my journey with RoboISM began. Till that point of time, I almost had no idea about robotics. I had learnt C programming in junior college, but actually seeing some hardware respond to a program was absolutely fascinating for me. So, I went ahead and joined RoboISM. Getting selected wasn’t a big deal at all, back then, although these days, it has become a bit challenging.

SAC 203 - A practically empty room with just some wooden scrap, malfunctioning electronic components and tangled electrical wires lying in one corner. Yes, that was RoboISM during my first year. In the second year, my batchmates and I started working towards improving the club. With only five club members left, from my batch, it was indeed a tedious task. Firstly, we needed a logo, and we got a great design from the same person who later designed the logo for FotoFreaks. Being a small team, the tasks of planning and conducting workshops, guiding juniors, organizing robotics events in Concetto, and continually pushing the college administration for permissions and facilities, left us with very little time for working on our skills and projects. So, I began using the preparation time for exams for developing my projects. That did take a toll on my academic performance, but the sacrifice was worth it. Academics v/s club activities remains a debatable topic. For me, RoboISM came above everything else. But still, I maintained a decent GPA of around 8.4 because that too is important in its own ways.

During the third year, the club had begun to show signs of improvement. We now had an energetic and efficient team of sophomores and some brilliant freshers, who were way more skilled than us. Also that year, the robotics in Concetto events reached new heights. Lack of funding was still an issue, but that too got solved in my final year. Our juniors secured approval from the institute to purchase several robotics components, and RoboISM was also assigned a bigger club room (SAC 207). That year, we again got another amazing set of freshers, and as an icing on the cake, some members of the club also won a gold medal at Inter-IIT Tech Meet. Of course, there was still more to do, but having things fall into place during my last year at the club was the best farewell I could get.

RoboISM has been instrumental in the choice of my career. Robotics itself is a vast field, and understanding its subdomains is crucial. Being a part of a like-minded peer group helped me gain exposure to many spheres without diving deep into them myself. During the second and third year, I explored a variety of fields like steganography, computer vision, robotic manipulation, embedded systems, and IR communication. That’s how I realized that embedded systems are my true passion. I feel that the first three years of engineering are the ones for finding which domain works well for you. That is the time for making wrong decisions, learning from them and rectifying your choices accordingly.

By the end of the final year, none of my applications for higher studies had succeeded. And I did not have a job offer either. However, the projects developed at the club, and the technical skills acquired, had built a strong profile for me. I started looking for a job after I left college, and within a month, I managed to receive two offers. RoboISM had fostered my knowledge to such an extent, that things went good even though I didn’t have much time to prepare for the interviews. That’s one excellent thing about the club. In the process of discussing with your peers and teaching juniors, you unknowingly strengthen your understanding. Sometimes, juniors raise doubts which force you to think in a new direction. RoboISM has helped me recognise that teaching is the best way of learning.

When I started working in the embedded systems industry, I realised that the technologies in use and the level of products were much superior to those which I had worked on, at RoboISM. But, even though the knowledge and experience gained at the club did not meet industry standards, it still is very much necessary. It helped me a great deal in quickly getting acquainted with more advanced systems. It has been correctly said that the secret of getting ahead is getting started. And RoboISM provided me with that essential kickstart.

Now, this is for the present club members. In 2016, when we decided to uplift the club, I was well aware that this process could take years and I might graduate before witnessing it. As times changed, the next batches had different goals than ours. But at the end of the day, we all wanted to make the club a better place for future students. We did not want you guys to face the problems which we went through. Of course, as the club progresses, there are always going to be new difficulties. At times, you might lose hope or feel like giving up. That is when you need to give it a moment and think, “Is the club better today, than it was at the time I joined this college?” If the answer is yes, which would most likely be the case, then that definitely is a success. Instead of quitting, you need to navigate the club through those hurdles and be ready to face the next set of challenges. Always have faith in RoboISM and its family, serve it with your heart and soul, and think beyond infinity!!
